Suicides by GDP

The scatter plot shows that there's no correlation between suicide rates and gdp per capita.
However, the line chart tells a different story. Except for the first ten years, the two line charts moved in an opposite direction which may imply that the GDP has an effect on the suicide rates.
This negative correlation can be explained by the economic development as well as improved national mental health policies and infrastructures

To conclude, an economic growth without adequate infrastructures for mental health services is the key to lowering suicide rates.
